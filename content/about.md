My old boss at CNN Interactive — Harry Motro — asked me an innocuous question in my interview that I later decided was quite meaningful.

*In one sentence: what drives you? What gets you out of bed in the morning?*

I said: I like to make cool things.

What I think is cool has changed over time. I've become more driven by people and culture than technology. But the basic drive to make something new; something that delights its intended audience is still the thing that jazzes me.

Thirty years in tech. Engineer first, product leader since. I've helped launch CNN.com, built programmatic advertising infrastructure at Turner, grown a fintech app to a million users, and spent the last few years deep in questions about AI and what we owe the people affected by what we build.

I'm based in Atlanta. I mentor product managers, advise on fractional engagements, and DJ dance music for old people on the side.
